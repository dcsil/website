Apply as a Student

These courses are balloted, which means you need to submit an application.

You will need a PDF of your most recent unofficial transcript from ROSI/Acorn (i.e. a full academic history), a PDF of your up-to-date resume/CV and answers to a set of ballot courses.

What are we looking for?

Typically students who are accepted have:

  • Passion
  • A learn-as-you-go attitude
  • Usually have a good GPA, although there is no set minimum
  • Have software experience for CSC491/2600
  • Show entrepreneurial drive
  • Willing to present complex and novel ideas

Apply to be a TA

We are looking for TAs that generally have startup and software experience.

These candidates should bring unique perspectives to the courses.

We use the standard application process for the University of Toronto.

What are we looking for?

Ideal TA candidates typically have:

  • Startup experience or entrepreneurial background
  • Strong software development skills
  • Ability to mentor and guide student teams
  • Experience with customer development or product development
  • Excellent communication and collaboration skills
  • Passion for teaching and supporting student learning

Be the next Industry Partner

Partner with DCSIL to provide real-world challenges for our student teams. Industry partners work directly with students to solve actual business problems and innovation opportunities.

You'll collaborate with talented students from across the University of Toronto who bring fresh perspectives and technical expertise to your organization's challenges.

What are the benefits?

Industry partners gain:

  • Access to innovative solutions for technical challenges
  • Direct collaboration with top-tier student talent
  • Opportunity to identify and recruit future employees
  • Working products and technical documentation
  • Fresh perspectives on business and technology challenges

Become a Guest Speaker

Share your expertise and entrepreneurial journey with DCSIL students. Guest speakers provide invaluable insights from their experience building and scaling technology companies.

These sessions inspire, challenge, and provide practical knowledge that students can immediately apply to their own projects and ventures.

What are the opportunities?

Guest speakers contribute to:

  • Inspiring the next generation of entrepreneurs
  • Sharing real-world experiences and lessons learned
  • Providing strategic and technical insights
  • Building connections with talented students
  • Contributing to the DCSIL community
